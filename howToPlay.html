How to play

Basics


Controls:
-Left click to place tetrads - press spacebar to rotate the orientation of the upcoming tetrad.
-The goal of the game is to prevent the enemies from getting to the last waypoint. You win the game once all enemies have spawned and disappeared off the map (either killed or reaching the last waypoint) and you still have lives remaining.
-The enemies spawn right next to the waypoint labelled "1", and move sequentially between waypoints. You can place tetrads on the map which will both lengthen the path enemies have to take between waypoints, and attack enemies. You are not allowed to place tetrads that will block the enemies path between waypoints.
-The red lines between waypoints show the path that enemies will walk along between the tetrads, in order to make it easier for you to construct your maze.
-The placement timer in the top right shows how long you have to wait before you'll be able to place your next tetrad.

Mega Tetrads



If you place four tetrads in a configuration that creates the shape of a larger tetrad, you will create a superior "mega tetrad". You must use exactly four smaller tetrads to create the mega tetrad, but other than that, anything's game! Mega tetrads are more powerful versions of their small counterparts. See Tetrad Types section for details.

There are seven tetrad types:

Range: 50
Special: Slows enemies

Special: N/A

Damage:100
Attack Cooldown: 50
Range: 20
Special: N/A


L

Damage:40
Attack Cooldown: 100
Range: 10
Special: Attacks all enemies in range at once


J

Damage:40
Attack Cooldown: 100
Range: 10

T

Damage:45
Attack Cooldown: 20
Range: 10

Mega tetrads have similar attributes to their smaller versions but with:

-Triple the damage
-One third the attack cooldown(rounded up)
-Triple the range
-Slowing effect lasts twice as long(for O tetrads)
